About

Josh Tycko develops high-throughput synthetic biology technologies to measure and manipulate human gene regulation. He is a postdoctoral fellow at Harvard Medical School, in the labs of Michael Greenberg and Martin Pollak.

He earned his Ph.D. in Genetics at Stanford University with Michael Bassik and Lacra Bintu, supported by an NIH F99/K00 fellowship and the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship. There he built pooled assays that read out the functions of thousands of human protein domains, mapping transcriptional effectors and engineering compact tools to switch genes on and off.

He is also a scientific co-founder of Stylus Medicine, which is translating recombinase-based genome integration into in vivo therapies.

Research

Interests

  1. Gene and chromatin regulation

    Discovering transcriptional effector domains across the human proteome at scale, and engineering improved activators and repressors for synthetic biology.

  2. Gene delivery

    Finding recombinases and viral vectors that write large DNA sequences into the human genome for durable genetic medicine.

  3. Genetic and epigenetic editing

    Building CRISPR tools to interrogate the noncoding genome, and measuring and mitigating off-target effects to make safer therapies.
